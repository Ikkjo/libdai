\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
A \doxylink{classdai_1_1RegionGraph}{Region\+Graph} combines a bipartite graph consisting of outer regions (type \doxylink{classdai_1_1FRegion}{FRegion}) and inner regions (type \doxylink{classdai_1_1Region}{Region}) with a \doxylink{classdai_1_1FactorGraph}{Factor\+Graph}. 

A \doxylink{classdai_1_1RegionGraph}{Region\+Graph} inherits from a \doxylink{classdai_1_1FactorGraph}{Factor\+Graph} and adds additional structure in the form of a "{}region graph"{}. Our definition of region graph is inspired by \mbox{[}\doxylink{bibliography_HAK03}{HAK03}\mbox{]}, which is less general than the definition given in \mbox{[}\doxylink{bibliography_YFW05}{YFW05}\mbox{]}.

The extra structure described by a \doxylink{classdai_1_1RegionGraph}{Region\+Graph} compared with that described by a \doxylink{classdai_1_1FactorGraph}{Factor\+Graph} is\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item a set of outer regions (indexed by $\alpha$), where each outer region consists of
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item a factor defined on a subset of variables
\item a counting number
\end{DoxyItemize}
\item a set of inner regions (indexed by $\beta$), where each inner region consists of
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item a subset of variables
\item a counting number
\end{DoxyItemize}
\item edges between inner and outer regions
\end{DoxyItemize}

Each factor in the factor graph belongs to an outer region; normally, the factor contents of an outer region would be the product of all the factors that belong to that region. \begin{DoxyRefDesc}{Idea}
\item[\mbox{\hyperlink{ideas__ideas000014}{Idea}}]Generalize the definition of region graphs to the one given in \mbox{[}\doxylink{bibliography_YFW05}{YFW05}\mbox{]}, i.\+e., replace the current implementation which uses a \doxylink{classdai_1_1BipartiteGraph}{Bipartite\+Graph} with one that uses a \doxylink{classdai_1_1DAG}{DAG}. 

The outer regions are products of factors; right now, this product is constantly cached\+: changing one factor results in an update of all relevant outer regions. This may not be the most efficient approach; an alternative would be to only precompute the factor products at the start of an inference algorithm -\/ e.\+g., in init(). This has the additional advantage that \doxylink{classdai_1_1FactorGraph}{Factor\+Graph} e can offer write access to its factors. \end{DoxyRefDesc}

Constructs a region graph from a factor graph and a vector of outer clusters (CVM style) 

The region graph is constructed as in the Cluster Variation Method.

The outer regions have as variable subsets the clusters specified in {\itshape cl}. Each factor in the factor graph {\itshape fg} is assigned to one of the outer regions. Each outer region gets counting number 1.

The inner regions are (repeated) intersections of outer regions. An inner and an outer region are connected if the variables in the inner region form a subset of the variables in the outer region. The counting numbers for the inner regions are calculated by \doxylink{classdai_1_1FactorGraph_a7a52762858a64e68666d455becded4c1}{calc\+Counting\+Numbers()} and satisfy the Moebius formula.
